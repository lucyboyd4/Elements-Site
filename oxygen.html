Background +

History of the element

Backgroundx

Oxygen is a non-metal element and is found naturally as a molecule. Each molecule is made up of two oxygen atoms that are strongly joined together. Oxygen has low melting and boiling points, so it is in a gas state at room temperature. It is a member of the chalcogen group on the periodic table and is an oxidizing agent that readily forms oxides with most elements as well as with other compounds.

Who and what?

Uses and Discoveryx

Oxygen is vital for respiration, which is the process that transfers energy from glucose to cells. It is also necessary for burning to occur. This will only happen when the mixture of fuel and oxygen is hot enough.

In 1772 Oxygen was discovered by a Swedish Chemist called Carl Wilhelm Scheele
