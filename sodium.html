Background

Backgroundx

Sodium is a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive metal. It is an alkali metal because it has a single electron in its outer shell that it readily donates, creating a positively charged ion. When isolated in metallic form it immediately oxidises upon contact with air, and violently produces hydrogen gas which may burst into flame when brought into contact with water. It is one of the highly reactive elements in its group.

Uses and Discovery

Uses and Discoveryx

The most common compound of sodium is sodium chloride (table salt). It is added to food and used to de-ice roads in winter. Sodium vapor is used in streetlights and produces a brilliant yellow light.

In 1807 Sodium was discovered by Sir Humphry Davy.
